10/1/21

Capture the Flag - Walmart Cyber Range

9/2/20 - 9/5/20

"A technology that is 100% functional has 0% security, and a technology that is 100% secure has 0% functionality"

Capture the Flag was a unique event and introduced me to a lot of new topics in Cybersecurity from the perspective of a hacker. We used BurpSuite for Web Proxy, SQL tables for SQL injection, data validation error, and many more forms of attacks to hack into and find vulnerabilities in a fake e-commerce website JuiceShop. I gained exposure in navigating the Kali Linux OS which I found new and fun. The most significant idea I learned was that Cybersecurity is present in every field of technology because every technology has to find the right balance between functionality and security. This has also made me more conscious of my privacy and security in the technologies I use everyday.

Utilization Dashboard - Toshiba Kristi Weiser

10/19/20 - 10/30/20

Selected winner of Afternoon Ignite class

In the Toshiba project we in the Technology strand were tasked with coming up with a real-world, practical solution and pitch for the problem of visualizing tables of diverse data in a simple dashboard. I learned how to work as a group as we implemented a mix of allocated tasks and creative group brainstorming as we developed our solution. We had to manage our time effectively as we had a fixed two week time period. We had to work on our presentation skills and engage the judge to "sell our product." Overall, it was a wonderful experience that taught me valuable skills along with the joy of winning as the best solution in our class. Mrs. Weiser provided a variety of positve feedback and constructive criticism for us to improve upon. One advice I will work on was to simplify the visual representation, but also focus more on more creative, innovative solutions that can be showcased. Overall, I am glad I was able to participate in this competitive, purposeful event and look forward to competing in more related events.
